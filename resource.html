Laws concerning women's safety and crimes against them:

  • The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013: This amendment to the Indian Penal Code (IPC), the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), and the Indian Evidence Act introduced several changes to strengthen laws related to sexual offenses, including rape, sexual harassment, and acid attacks. It expanded the definition of rape, increased penalties for offenders, and introduced new offenses like stalking and voyeurism.

  • The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005: This law provides for the protection of women from domestic violence and abuse, including physical, emotional, verbal, sexual, and economic abuse. It enables women to seek protection orders, residence orders, and financial relief, and it imposes penalties on perpetrators.

  • The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013: This legislation aims to prevent and address sexual harassment of women in the workplace. It mandates the establishment of internal complaints committees in organizations and outlines procedures for complaints, inquiries, and penalties for non-compliance.

  • The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961: This law prohibits the giving or receiving of dowry in connection with marriage. It aims to prevent dowry-related harassment and violence against women, which continues to be a significant issue in many parts of India.

  • The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006: This act prohibits the solemnization of child marriages and provides for the annulment of child marriages and protection and rehabilitation of victims. It aims to prevent early and forced marriages, which disproportionately affect girls and violate their rights.

  • The Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986: This law prohibits the depiction of women in a derogatory or exploitative manner in advertisements, publications, or other media. It aims to prevent the objectification and exploitation of women's bodies for commercial purposes.

  • The National Commission for Women (NCW): Established in 1992, the NCW is a statutory body tasked with promoting and protecting the rights of women in India. It investigates complaints of gender-based discrimination and violence, conducts studies and research on women's issues, and recommends legislative and policy measures for women's empowerment and safety.

  • The Protection of Children from Sexual Offenses (POCSO) Act, 2012: This law is specifically designed to protect children from sexual abuse and exploitation. It defines various forms of sexual offenses against children and prescribes stringent penalties for offenders. It also includes provisions for the establishment of special courts and procedures to ensure the speedy trial of cases.

  • The Prohibition of Trafficking in Persons Act, 2018: This legislation addresses human trafficking, including trafficking for the purpose of sexual exploitation, forced labor, or other forms of exploitation. It provides for the prevention, rescue, and rehabilitation of victims, as well as the prosecution of traffickers.

  • The Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971: This law regulates the termination of pregnancies in India and aims to protect women's reproductive health and rights. It allows for abortions under certain conditions, such as when the pregnancy poses a risk to the woman's life or health, or in cases of fetal abnormalities.

  • The Maternity Benefit Act, 1961: This act aims to ensure the welfare of women workers during pregnancy and childbirth by providing maternity leave, maternity benefits, and other related provisions. It applies to women employed in factories, mines, plantations, shops, and other establishments.

  • The Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956: This law addresses trafficking for the purpose of prostitution and other forms of sexual exploitation. It prohibits brothel keeping, soliciting, and living off the earnings of prostitution, and provides for penalties for offenders and rehabilitation for victims.

  • The Domestic Workers Welfare and Social Security Act (Draft): This proposed legislation seeks to regulate the employment of domestic workers and ensure their rights, including fair wages, working conditions, and access to social security benefits. It aims to protect the rights and dignity of domestic workers, a significant portion of whom are women.

  • The Equal Remuneration Act, 1976: This act prohibits discrimination in wages on the basis of gender and ensures equal pay for equal work. It aims to address the gender pay gap and promote gender equality in employment and remuneration.
